How people now look up heritage

Tourists, students, and the public ask AI first.

What HeriTrace measures: when an answer sounds plausible, but quietly drops the heritage facts that matter for stewardship, attribution, and reuse.
Observed on our Cyprus cases: omission of licence terms, omission of the digitising institution, omission of historical phases, and weaker grounding on UNESCO-era context.
  • If Europeana has the fact and AI omits it, the institution loses visibility.
  • If Europeana lacks the field, the metadata gap becomes measurable and actionable.
  • If two sources disagree, curators get a review signal instead of a vague complaint.

Why this matters to judges

What we built

A data curation platform for the age of AI.

HeriTrace ingests Europeana and Twin it! records, compares prepared mainstream AI answers against institutional ground truth, classifies what went wrong, and produces a report that curators can actually act on.

1

Ingest

Use real Europeana-grounded metadata for the demo objects instead of a pure mock layer.

2

Compare

Check captured provider outputs against the same fields a curator would trust: title, context, place, rights, and provenance clues.

3

Explain

Classify mismatches, AI omissions, Europeana metadata gaps, and cross-source disagreements instead of giving a vague score.

4

Report

Surface executive summary first, then drill down to the object where a heritage professional can review the exact evidence.

Current dev scope

  • Five demo objects, including Cyprus heritage from Twin it!
  • Three providers with fifteen prepared response files on dev
  • Live report and asset drill-down flow on `heritrace.dev.hackathon.inics.ltd`

Roadmap boundary

  • We do not claim live provider benchmarking in the demo.
  • We do not claim Europeana-wide scale yet.
  • Watermarking and provenance linkage stay in the roadmap, not in this MVP story.

Roadmap after review

Scale the evidence loop from 5 objects to Europeana-wide curation.

  • Expand beyond the Cyprus demo set toward broader Europeana and Twin it! coverage.
  • Automate institution-facing correction reports so metadata teams can act faster.
  • Support fair-terms dataset stewardship for future AI training readiness.
  • Later reconnect provenance and watermarking to track not only visibility, but reuse and remix.

Why each judge should care

UNESCO / stewardship

Digitisation is only the first half of the mission. HeriTrace shows whether heritage stays accurate and attributable when the public encounters it through AI.

Europeana / metadata quality

The product turns metadata completeness and reuse quality into visible, prioritised work instead of an abstract standards discussion.

EU Commission / policy value

Public investment in 3D heritage should produce discoverability, reuse, and measurable digital impact. HeriTrace provides the reporting layer for that question.
